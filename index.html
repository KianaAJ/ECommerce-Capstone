All About the synths

The History Of Synthwaves

Synthwave is a music genre that emerged in the mid-2000s, characterized by its nostalgic homage to the sounds and aesthetics of 1980s electronic and pop culture. It draws inspiration from the synthesizer-driven soundtracks of iconic movies and video games from that era, as well as the pulsating beats of early electronic dance music. Synthwave artists often use vintage synthesizers and drum machines to create a retro-futuristic sound that evokes a sense of nostalgia for a bygone era. The genre gained popularity through online platforms and social media, with artists like Kavinsky, College, and Miami Nights 1984 contributing to its rise. Synthwave's blend of nostalgia and modern production techniques has since spawned numerous subgenres and a dedicated fan base, making it a prominent and enduring part of contemporary electronic music culture.

Fun Facts About Synthwave

Neon Aesthetics: Synthwave art often features a prominent neon color palette, with vibrant pinks, blues, and purples. This choice of colors is a nod to the neon signs and futuristic visuals of the 1980s. 80s Pop Culture References: Synthwave artists frequently incorporate references to iconic 1980s pop culture, including movies like "Blade Runner" and "The Terminator," as well as video games like "Outrun." These references resonate with fans of the era. Retro Futurism: Synthwave art captures the essence of retro-futurism, blending the optimism of how people in the '80s envisioned the future with the technology of the time. It often features elements like futuristic cityscapes, retro cars, and high-tech gadgets. VHS Tape Aesthetics: To evoke the feel of the '80s, synthwave art sometimes incorporates VHS tape glitches, tracking lines, and scan errors. This adds an authentic retro touch to the visuals.

The Difference Between Vaporwave and Synthwave
  • Vaporwave-Vaporwave, on the other hand, is known for its slowed-down, often chill and mellow sound. It heavily samples music from the '80s and '90s, with an emphasis on smooth jazz, elevator music, and corporate muzak. Vaporwave tends to be more experimental, abstract, and ambient in its musical approach.
  • Synthwave-Synthwave, also known as retrowave or outrun, is characterized by its nostalgic homage to the electronic music of the 1980s. It often features a retro-futuristic sound with prominent synthesizers, drum machines, and '80s-inspired melodies. The music aims to capture the essence of that era's pop culture, emphasizing energetic, often upbeat tracks.
